How to compare Midland plans the right way

Why compare by your address

Midland rates and plan availability vary by ZIP and by how much power your home actually uses, so an average misleads. The price that looks low on paper can shift once the plan is matched to your real usage.

  • Availability differs by ZIP across Midland and the Oncor area.
  • Usage changes the winner. A small apartment and a large West Texas house rarely land on the same plan.

What to check on each plan

  • Price at 500 / 1,000 / 2,000 kWh: matched to your home size.
  • Minimum-usage fees: charges if you use too little.
  • Bill credits: the usage level where they kick in.
  • Term & early-termination fee: what leaving early costs.
  • Renewable content: the green percentage, if it matters to you.

No. Oncor keeps delivering power without interruption while your new plan simply begins billing.

Who is the Transmission and Distribution Utility (TDU) in Midland?

Oncor is the Transmission and Distribution Utility for the Midland area. They manage the lines, outages, and delivery regardless of your retail plan.
