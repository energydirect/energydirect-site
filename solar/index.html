How a solar buyback plan works

How solar buyback works

  • You generate power: your rooftop panels produce electricity for your home during the day.
  • You use what you need: your home draws from your panels first.
  • You export the excess: extra power your home does not use flows back onto the grid.
  • The plan credits you: a solar buyback plan credits you for that exported energy on your bill.

Your panels and your retail plan are two separate things. The plan sets how your exported energy is credited and how you are billed for the power you pull from the grid at night or on cloudy days.

What to compare on a solar plan

  • Buyback structure: how the plan credits the energy you export back to the grid.
  • Export caps: whether credits are limited to a certain amount of exported energy.
  • Term and early-termination fee: the contract length and what leaving early costs.
  • Monthly and minimum-usage fees: any base charges or fees if you draw very little from the grid.
  • Renewable content: the green percentage, if it matters to you.

Plans and availability vary by ZIP, so a smart comparison starts with your address, not a single statewide number. Always read a plan's Electricity Facts Label before you enroll.

Texas solar buyback questions

What is a solar buyback plan?

It is a retail electricity plan that credits you for the excess solar energy your home exports to the grid. You still draw power from the grid at night or on cloudy days, and the plan sets how that energy is billed and how your exports are credited.

Do I need rooftop solar already installed?

Yes. A solar buyback plan credits the energy your panels send back to the grid, so it is for homes that already have rooftop solar. If you are still planning your system, you can compare standard plans now and switch when your panels are online.

Will switching to a solar plan interrupt my power?

No. Your local utility keeps delivering power without interruption while your new plan simply begins billing. Your panels and your meter keep working exactly as before.

Is there any cost to compare or to get help?

No. Comparing plans and getting help from Energy Direct is free. You enroll directly in the plan you choose.
