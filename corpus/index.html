How electricity choice works in Corpus Christi

Corpus Christi is in AEP Texas Central’s delivery territory. The TDU maintains poles and wires, while you pick a retail electricity provider for your plan. Whether you live in Calallen, Flour Bluff, or Padre Island, plan availability and pricing can vary by ZIP code and usage.

Coastal humidity and long cooling seasons can increase summer usage. Use the EFL to compare pricing at 500 / 1000 / 2000 kWh, and check for any bill credits, base charges, or minimum usage fees.

What to look for in the EFL

  • Average price at 500/1000/2000 kWh
  • Base charges or minimum usage fees
  • Bill credits and threshold levels
  • Contract length and early termination fee
  • Renewable content

Corpus Christi FAQs

Who handles outages?
AEP Texas manages delivery and outage restoration, regardless of provider. Visit the AEP Texas outage center or check the outage map.

Are time-of-use plans available?
Some providers offer plans with on-peak/off-peak pricing. Check the EFL to see if it fits your routine.

Will switching interrupt my power?
No—switching providers is seamless, and your lights stay on during the change. For assistance, see AEP Texas customer support.
